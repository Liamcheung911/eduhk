Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in
Global and Environmental Studies
(Four-year Full-time)

Programme Code: JS8624


Programme Aims

This programme aims to cultivate students as caring intellectuals who can analyse contemporary global and environmental affairs from multiple disciplinary and cultural perspectives; communicate proficiently; work effectively both independently and in teams; reflect critically on their identities and responsibilities as local, national, and global and ecological citizens; demonstrate sensitivity to and respect for cultural diversity and concern for enhancing human well-being; and make active contributions to local, national, and global society and environment improvement.

Programme Features

This is an interdisciplinary, cross-cultural programme designed to provide students with an in-depth understanding of the increasing interactions amongst globalisation, environmental change, and economic, political, and social developments, as well as strong critical-thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills. The unique features of the programme include the following.

  • In-depth, integrated understanding:
    The programme focuses on the study of globalisation, economic development and environmental change. All aspects of the programme contribute to the goal of providing students with in-depth, integrated understanding of these global phenomena.
  • Interdisciplinary approach:
    The programme takes a holistic, interdisciplinary approach, looking at global and environmental issues from the perspectives of political science, sociology, economics, science and technology, and philosophy.
  • Global phenomena, local action:
    The programme is based on the idea that globalisation, economic development and environmental change face all societies, while they affect and are managed in different ways in different societies. The programme's coursework helps students to analyse these concepts at the global and international level, while experiential learning involves students in action in local, practical contexts.
  • Experiential learning:
    A large portion of students' time in this programme will be spent in non-classroom settings, engaging them in cross-cultural dialogue with students from other Asian or Western societies and in practical work that helps them to put their knowledge and skills into practice.
  • Integrated work:
    Advanced courses in the programme require students to integrate and apply their knowledge and skills to analyse specific case studies of globalisation, development, and environmental change. The final Honours Project builds on all components of the programme, including coursework, the internship and other experiential learning, and research preparation, to complete a substantial project which analyses a relevant problem and proposes solutions.
  • Diversified graduation pathways:
    The elements of choice built into the programme through Electives, Minors and Internship placements enhance students' ability to pursue a variety of careers in government, non-governmental organisations, concern groups, consultancies, international business, journalism, and other fields requiring global and environmental literacy. The Sustainability Studies Minor extends students' understanding of their Major and offers diversified graduation pathways related to urban studies, cultural management, and governance and policy or, alternatively, scientific or technical careers in environment and sustainability. Students are also prepared to undertake further studies or pursue a teaching career by completing an additional year of professional teacher training.

Major Studies

The Global and Environment Studies Major comprises the following:

  • Foundation Courses: Concepts and Aspects of Global and Environmental Studies
  • Critical Analysis in Global and Environment Studies
  • Global and Environment Challenges: Regional and International Responses
  • Applied Global and Environmental Studies

Major Coursework begins with Foundation Courses introducing students to key social science and science concepts relevant to later stages of the programme. It also introduces students to the problems of globalisation, development, and environmental change. Building upon this foundation, students are then engaged in critically analysing and proposing solutions to these global issues. In a more advanced stage, students are required to integrate their knowledge and skills through in-depth examination of specific cases of the interaction of globalisation, development, and environmental change in Hong Kong, mainland China, and other Asian and international societies. Additional courses in the later stages of the programme require students to apply their knowledge and skills in practical societal and work settings.

  • Regional Summer Institute
    Students are required and given support to participate in the Regional Summer Institute, an international exchange experience involving lectures, class debates, discussions, field visits, and professional visits to government offices, non-governmental organisations, and social service agencies in selected Asian societies together with students from those societies. It creates an interactive learning environment where international students and local students combine knowledge acquired from lectures and seminars with an understanding of real-world problems. Students are required to participate in the Regional Summer Institute in the summer between Years 2 and 3.
  • Internship
    Students will further cultivate skills and knowledge developed during coursework by participating in an Internship in a relevant organisation or company with an international orientation in the summer between Years 3 and 4. This experience will allow students to turn their classroom learning into practice in a real-life situation and will provide additional knowledge about global and environmental issues as they apply to the work setting.
  • International Exchange
    Students are also encouraged to embark on an overseas learning journey through participating in the international academic exchange programme or joining international summer school organised by overseas universities.
  • Final Year Project (Honours Project / Capstone Project)
    The Honours Project / Capstone Project is an individual major project on a topic of students' own choice related to the interactions among globalisation, development, and environmental change. It consists of two phases:
    • Phase I: Basic Research Methods, which provides students with training in conducting inquiry and research; and
    • Phase II: Honours Project / Capstone Project, which requires students to work closely with a project supervisor to complete a major piece of research and writing or a project.

Language Courses

Students in the programme are expected to develop the ability to communicate effectively in English and Chinese (including Putonghua). To assist them in meeting this goal, the Centre for Language in Education (CLE) will offer a comprehensive package of mandatory credit-bearing and mandatory non-credit bearing language enhancement activities, amounting to no less than 297 hours of taught courses and documented self-access study. This comprises 138 hours of English (including two 3-cp courses), 99 hours of Chinese (including one 3-cp course), and 60 hours of Putonghua, over the four years of the programme. The language enhancement package requires students to make use of the Arthur Samy Language Learning Centre (ASLLC) and related facilities available at the Tai Po campus.

Students may be exempted from selected Chinese, English or Putonghua courses in the package if they have fulfilled the exemption criteria of individual courses.

Non-Cantonese-speaking students will be required to study two non-credit bearing Cantonese courses. English-speaking students can study Cantonese and Putonghua courses.

Medium of Instruction

The programme will mainly be conducted in English. All Major courses will be taught in English. For some non-Major courses, Putonghua or Cantonese will be adopted as the medium of instruction when the language is considered to be most effective for teaching.

Career Prospects/Professional Qualifications

There is a growing community demand for people with a global perspective, cultural sensitivity, environmental literacy, and strong critical thinking, communication and problem-solving skills. This programme will fill this gap and help prepare graduates for a wide range of careers requiring global vision, environmental literacy, and an enhanced understanding of how the wider world affects Hong Kong and China. Graduates are well-prepared to work in government, non-governmental organisations, international businesses, environmental consultancies and other professions.
